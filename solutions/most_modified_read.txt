We've detailed a solution below.
There may be other ways of solving the problem.
We interpret 'most modified' to mean highest total number of modifications
in the read and present a solution below.
However, you can interpret it as the read with the
highest mean modification density or any other reasonable interpretation.

The commands to find reads with more than 100 modifications is shown below;
we've discussed this command earlier in the course.
Just keep increasing 100 till you hit a number where there is only one read left
(this number is around 8000).

```bash
input_mod_bam=~/nanomod_course_data/human/bonito_calls.subset.sorted.bam
nanalogue read-info --tag m $input_mod_bam |  jq -r '.[] | {read_id: .read_id,
                mod_count: (.mod_count | split(":")[1] | split(";")[0] | tonumber )}
             | select(.mod_count > 100) | .read_id'
```
